## [Canada plans up to 10 new nuclear reactors in 15 years](https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/federal-nuclear-strategy-9.7244509) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The Canadian government has announced a plan to build up to 10 new nuclear reactors over the next 15 years, leveraging its abundant uranium reserves and expertise in CANDU reactor technology. This marks a significant shift in Canada's energy policy, potentially providing reliable baseload power to complement renewable sources like solar and wind, while also supporting industrial decarbonization and climate goals. The plan includes both traditional CANDU reactors and small modular reactors (SMRs), with the Darlington New Nuclear Project already under construction. However, previous advanced CANDU designs failed to find buyers, and legislative delays remain a concern.

hackernews · geox · Jun 22, 19:06 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48634585)

**Background**: The CANDU (Canada Deuterium Uranium) reactor is a Canadian pressurized heavy-water reactor design that uses natural uranium fuel without enrichment. Developed in the 1950s-60s, it has been exported to several countries, with 26 operational units worldwide as of 2026. Canada also established an SMR roadmap in 2017 to explore smaller, modular nuclear technologies.

## [Deno Desktop: Build Desktop Apps with Multiple Rendering Backends](https://docs.deno.com/runtime/desktop/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Deno has introduced desktop application support in version 2.9.0 (currently canary), allowing developers to build native desktop apps using Deno with three rendering backends: CEF (Chromium Embedded Framework), Webview (WebView2 on Windows, WebKit on macOS/Linux), and Raw (no UI). This feature significantly expands Deno's applicability from server-side and CLI tools to desktop development, offering a lightweight alternative to Electron with shared runtime potential and integration with Deno's permission system. The shared CEF runtime is on the roadmap to reduce binary sizes to a few MB per app; permissions granted at compile time are baked into the binary, and the community suggests surfacing them to users at runtime.

hackernews · GeneralMaximus · Jun 22, 05:38 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48626137)

**Background**: Deno is a JavaScript/TypeScript runtime built on V8, Rust, and Tokio, known for its security-first approach with explicit permissions. Desktop app development traditionally requires bundling a browser engine (like Electron with Chromium), leading to large binary sizes. Deno Desktop aims to solve this with a shared runtime model.
