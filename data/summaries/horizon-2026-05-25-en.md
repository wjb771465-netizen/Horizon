## [California moves to exempt Linux from age-verification law after backlash](https://www.tomshardware.com/software/linux/california-moves-to-exempt-linux-from-its-upcoming-age-verification-law-after-backlash-over-forcing-operating-systems-to-collect-users-ages-amendment-proposed-by-the-same-lawmaker-who-wrote-the-original-law) ⭐️ 8.0/10

California has proposed an amendment to its upcoming age-verification law to exempt Linux and other open-source operating systems from the requirement to collect users' ages. The amendment was introduced by the same lawmaker who authored the original law, following backlash over the burden it would place on open-source platforms. This move is significant because it prevents a potentially crippling compliance burden on Linux and open-source operating systems, which are foundational to much of the internet and modern computing. If left unaddressed, the law could have stifled innovation and forced open-source projects to implement costly age-verification mechanisms, affecting millions of users worldwide. The original law would have required any device or operating system that can access the internet to verify users' ages, which critics argued would impose an impossible burden on open-source systems like Linux. The amendment specifically exempts operating systems that are not primarily designed for commercial distribution or that do not have a centralized entity to enforce compliance.

hackernews · rbanffy · May 25, 18:19 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48269961)

**Background**: California's age-verification law is part of a broader push to protect minors online, but it has drawn criticism for its broad scope and potential to undermine privacy and open-source development. Linux, as a free and open-source operating system, is maintained by a decentralized community of developers, making centralized age-verification impractical. The backlash highlighted tensions between child safety regulations and the principles of open-source software.

**Discussion**: Community comments expressed skepticism about the law's motives, with some suggesting the exemption might be a strategic move to prevent Linux developers from challenging the law on First Amendment grounds. Others criticized the law for burdening consumers instead of regulating companies, and noted that even with the exemption, SteamOS could still be affected if it is considered a commercial distribution.

## [Pope Leo XIV's Encyclical on AI Ethics](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Pope Leo XIV released the encyclical 'Magnifica Humanitas' on May 15, 2026, which examines the ethical implications of modern technology, including artificial intelligence, and calls for a human-centered approach to technological development. This encyclical represents a major institutional voice from the Vatican addressing the ethical challenges of AI and technology, potentially influencing global discourse on technology ethics and human dignity. It underscores the growing recognition that technology development must consider broader societal and spiritual values. The encyclical argues that technology is never neutral, as it reflects the characteristics of those who devise, finance, regulate, and use it. It places particular ethical and spiritual responsibility on builders and developers of technology, urging them to consider the impact of their creations on civilization.

**Background**: An encyclical is a formal letter from the Pope addressed to the Catholic Church and often to the wider world, outlining the Church's position on important issues. This encyclical builds on previous Vatican statements on technology, including Pope Francis's warnings about the concentration of power through technologies like nuclear energy, biotechnology, and information technology.

## [Netherlands Seizes 800 Servers, Arrests 2 for Aiding Cyberattacks](https://krebsonsecurity.com/2026/05/netherlands-seizes-800-servers-arrests-2-for-aiding-cyberattacks/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Dutch authorities seized 800 servers and arrested two individuals for providing bulletproof hosting infrastructure to cybercriminals, marking a major law enforcement operation against cyberattack facilitation. This operation disrupts a key enabler of global cybercrime, as bulletproof hosting allows criminals to launch attacks with impunity. It underscores the Netherlands' role as both a hub for illicit hosting and a target for law enforcement crackdowns. The seized servers were linked to front companies for Russian intelligence, not legitimate hosting businesses. The arrests follow similar international operations like Operation Synergia III, which sinkholed 45,000 IP addresses and seized over 1,000 servers.

hackernews · jruohonen · May 25, 13:56 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48266906)

**Background**: Bulletproof hosting refers to services that ignore complaints about illegal activities, such as botnet command-and-control or spam, often operating in jurisdictions with lenient laws. The Netherlands has historically been a hotspot for such hosting due to its robust internet infrastructure and permissive regulations, as seen with the notorious Cyberbunker data center.
